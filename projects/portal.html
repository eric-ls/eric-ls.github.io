Web Design Decal Student Portal

This summer, I started design and development on an online portal for students taking the class I teach, the Web Design Decal (WDD).

Problem

How do we enable WDD students to quickly and easily access course material?

The current class management system we were using, Canvas, was bloated, difficult to use, and slow. We weren't using many features, and simply needed a platform to distribute lecture schedules, show homework assignments, and allow submissions.

Here's a screenshot of the University software:

There were a couple of things that didn't work out for our class specifically. The Canvas software was:

  • Physically slow to load
  • Interface isnâ€™t very intuitive - takes long time to develop mental model
  • Accessing relevant lecture material is difficult

One of the main goals I kept on my mind was to design an interface that students could spend the least amount of time on. Less time spent on our website during lecture = more time learning!

Hypothesis

A clean and fast course website that doesn't distract students from learning course material.

Goals

  • Quick access of relevant class material, like lecture slides, assignments, and studio activities
  • Easy upload and preview of assignment submission
  • Keeping track of logistics like grades and attendance
  • Not be a barrier to learning!

Research

From the previous semesters teaching the course, I already had a guess for the main audience of our site:

  • students uploading homework on due dates
  • students accessing class slides + activities during lecture

As it stood, our lectures occurred on Thursdays, and I was able to validate my hypothesis using Google Analytics. The spikes below correspond to weekly lecture dates:
